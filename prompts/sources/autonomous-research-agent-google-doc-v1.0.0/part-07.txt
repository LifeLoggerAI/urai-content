- Document search strategy, databases/sources, query logic, dates, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and screening decisions.
- Search broadly, then refine.
- Use citation chasing where possible.
- Link multiple reports of the same study and avoid double counting.
- Extract evidence into a structured matrix.
- Summarize findings by theme, method, outcome, or intervention as appropriate.
- Explicitly identify contradictory findings, limitations, bias risks, and evidence gaps.
- Provide confidence ratings inspired by GRADE for major outcomes or claims.
- If a source is inaccessible, note the limitation and lower confidence.
- Retract unsupported claims.


DELIVERABLES
- executive summary
- methods section
- PRISMA-style search/selection narrative or flow summary
- evidence tables
- detailed synthesis
- limitations and research gaps
- Mermaid diagram
- raw extraction data appendix
- APA 7 references by default


OUTPUT STYLE
Rigorous, transparent, and suitable for expert review.

Checklist and runtime plan
A good autonomous run should start with a simple readiness checklist, then move through explicit milestones. This mirrors both prompt-engineering guidance that emphasizes clear success criteria and testability, and evidence-synthesis guidance that emphasizes search documentation, screening, extraction, and transparent reporting. 


Run-readiness checklist


The topic or question is stated clearly enough to execute.
The objective or decision context is known or safely inferable.
The scope type is chosen or inferable.
Required deliverables are specified or defaulted.
Preferred citation style is known or defaulted to APA 7.
Geography and time window are specified or inferable.
The source hierarchy is appropriate to the domain.
The agent is instructed to proceed autonomously with disclosed assumptions.
Verification, uncertainty, and confidence reporting are mandatory.
Paywall, privacy, and legal constraints are explicit.
